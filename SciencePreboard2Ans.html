Section A: Biology

1) Sites of Glucose Breakdown
Correct Option: (D) Cytoplasm and Oxygen deficient muscle cells.
Step (a): Glucose (6-carbon) → Pyruvate (3-carbon) occurs in Cytoplasm.
Step (b): Pyruvate → Lactic Acid occurs in Muscle Cells during lack of oxygen.
2) Bisexual Flower Characteristics
Correct Option: (C) (i) and (iii). Bisexual flowers contain both male (Stamen) and female (Pistil) reproductive parts and can perform self or cross-pollination.
[Image of the structure of a bisexual flower]
3) Dihybrid Cross F1 Generation
Correct Option: (C) 100% round and yellow. Parents: RRyy x rrYY. F1 Genotype: RrYy. Dominance makes them Round and Yellow.
4) Solar Energy Capture
Correct Option: (D) 99%. Green plants capture 1% of solar energy for food production; the remaining 99% is not converted into food.
5) Invalid Food Chains
Correct Option: (D) (ii) and (iv). These do not follow the natural 10% energy flow or predator-prey hierarchy.
6) Exchange Blood Vessels
Correct Option: (B) Capillaries. Their walls are one-cell thick to allow rapid diffusion of nutrients and gases.
7) Non-biodegradable Group
Correct Option: (C) DDT, Polyester, Glass. These cannot be decomposed by natural decomposers like bacteria or fungi.
8) Assertion & Reason: Heredity
Correct Option: (C) Assertion is true but Reason is false. A child has the basic design but is not an "exact" copy due to genetic variations during DNA copying.
9) Assertion & Reason: Nervous System
Correct Option: (A) Both A and R are true, and R is the correct explanation. The PNS (cranial and spinal nerves) facilitates communication between the CNS and the rest of the body.
10) Geotropism in Roots and Shoots
(a) Root: Positive Geotropism (grows toward gravity).
(b) Shoot: Negative Geotropism (grows away from gravity).
Stimulus: Gravity.
11) Gamete vs. Zygote
Gamete: Haploid (n) sex cell (Sperm/Ovum).
Zygote: Diploid (2n) cell formed after fertilization.
Significance: Gametes ensure constant chromosome numbers across generations; the zygote is the first cell of a new organism.
12) Seed Functions / Yeast Culture
(i) Seed coat: Protection. (ii) Cotyledon: Food storage. (iii) Radicle: Root. (iv) Plumule: Shoot.
OR: Yeast Culture: Mix 10g sugar in 100ml warm water, add yeast. Keep in a warm place. Observation: Bubbles of CO2 and smell of alcohol.
13) Response in Mimosa pudica (Touch-me-not)
(a) Communication: Electrochemical signals.
(b) Mechanism: Cells change shape by changing water content (Turgor Pressure).
(c) Difference: This is Nastic (independent of direction); tendrils are Tropic (direction-dependent growth).
14) Ozone Layer (O3)
(a) Function: Absorbs harmful UV radiation.
(b) Formation: O2 → O + O (under UV); O + O2 → O3.
(c) Depletion: Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs).
15) Photosynthesis Details
(a) Definition: Process of making glucose (C6H12O6) from CO2 and H2O.
(b) Equation: 6CO2 + 12H2O + Chlorophyll + Sunlight → C6H12O6 + 6O2 + 6H2O.
(c) Desert Plants: Take CO2 at night to form an intermediate; perform photosynthesis during the day.
OR: (i) Cloudy: Light decreases, rate drops. (ii) Dust on stomata: CO2 entry blocked, rate drops.
16) Binary Fission & Human Reproduction
(i) Mode: Binary Fission. (ii) Organism: Amoeba. (iii) Advantages: Fast, no mate required. (iv) Hydra: Budding.
OR: (i) No fertilization: Uterine lining sheds (Menstruation). (ii) Testis: In Scrotum for lower temp (2-3°C less than body). (iii) Surgery: Vasectomy (male), Tubectomy (female).

Section B: Chemistry

17) pH and Salt Nature
Correct Option: (C) Neutral. Reaction: Strong Acid (pH 1) + Strong Base (pH 13) → Neutral Salt + H2O.
18) Homologous Series Properties
Correct Option: (B) Melting point increases, solubility decreases. As molecular mass increases, intermolecular forces strengthen.
19) Amphoteric Metal (Zinc)
Correct Option: (C) Zinc (Zn) and Hydrogen (H2).
Reaction with Acid: Zn + H2SO4 → ZnSO4 + H2
Reaction with Base: Zn + 2NaOH → Na2ZnO2 + H2
20) Matching Metal Oxides
Correct Option: (A) (a)-(ii), (b)-(iii), (c)-(iv), (d)-(i).
Basic: Na2O, Acidic: CO2, Neutral: CO, Amphoteric: ZnO.
21) Balanced Equation Coefficients
Correct Option: (C) 3, 2, 1, 6.
Equation: 3CaCl2 + 2Na3PO4 → Ca3(PO4)2 + 6NaCl.
22) Treating Acidic Soil
Correct Option: (A) Slaked lime [Ca(OH)2]. It is a base that neutralizes acidity.
23) Double Displacement Pairs
Correct Option: (D) (i), (ii), (iii), (iv). All involve an exchange of ions between the reactants.
24) Assertion & Reason: Carbon Compounds
Correct Option: (A) Both A and R are true. Carbon's Tetravalency and Catenation allow it to form millions of stable covalent compounds.
25) Metal Reaction with Steam
(i) Metals: Aluminium (Al) or Iron (Fe).
(ii) Equations:
2Al + 3H2O(g) → Al2O3 + 3H2
3Fe + 4H2O(g) → Fe3O4 + 4H2
[Image of the action of steam on a metal]
26) Aluminium Nitride and Oxide
(a) AlN: Al (2,8,3) → Al3+ + 3e-. N (2,5) + 3e- → N3-. Product: AlN.
(b) Amphoteric Nature: Al2O3 reacts with both HCl and NaOH.
Al2O3 + 6HCl → 2AlCl3 + 3H2O
Al2O3 + 2NaOH → 2NaAlO2 + H2O
27) Chemical Reaction Equations
(a) Displacement: Zn + 2AgNO3 → Zn(NO3)2 + 2Ag
(b) Double Displacement: 2KI + Pb(NO3)2 → PbI2 + 2KNO3 (Yellow ppt)
(c) Decomposition: 2Pb(NO3)2 → 2PbO + 4NO2 + O2
28) Salts from Brine (NaCl)
(a) Caustic Soda (NaOH): Electrolysis of NaCl solution.
Baking Soda (NaHCO3): Solvay process: NaCl + H2O + CO2 + NH3 → NH4Cl + NaHCO3.
(b) Baking Powder: Contains Tartaric Acid to neutralize Na2CO3 (bitter) produced on heating.
OR: Nature of NaCl: Neutral. Formed from Strong Acid (HCl) and Strong Base (NaOH).
29) Ethanol and Carbon Chemistry
(a) Ethyne: C2H2; H-C≡C-H.
(b) Reactions:
1. 2C2H5OH + 2Na → 2C2H5ONa + H2
2. C2H5OH + CH3COOH → CH3COOC2H5 + H2O (Esterification)
(c) Oxidation: C2H5OH → CH3COOH (using Alkaline KMnO4).
OR: (a) Compound A: Ethanoic Acid (CH3COOH). (c) Gas: CO2.
(d) Saponification: CH3COOC2H5 + NaOH → CH3COONa + C2H5OH.

Section C: Physics

30) Speed of Light in Water
Correct Option: (A) 9/4 x 108 m/s.
c = ngvg = 1.5 x 2x108 = 3x108.
vw = c/nw = 3x108 / (4/3) = 9/4 x 108.
31) Scattering of Light
Correct Option: (C) Blue. Shorter wavelengths (blue/violet) are scattered most by fine particles in the atmosphere.
32) Magnetic Field Lines Assertion
Correct Option: (B) Both A and R are true, but R is not the correct explanation. Lines don't intersect because the resultant field cannot point in two directions at once.
33) Hypermetropia (Far-sightedness)
Causes: 1. Focal length too long. 2. Eyeball too short.
Correction: Convex lens of suitable power.

35) Resistance and Power
(a) Wire cut in 3: Each part = R/3. In parallel: 1/Req = 3/R + 3/R + 3/R = 9/R. Req = R/9.
(b) 1 Watt: Power when 1V causes a current of 1A. (P = VI).
36) Circular Loop Magnetic Field
(a) Lines: Concentric circles at the wire; straight lines at the center.
(b) Factors: Current magnitude and Radius of the loop.
(c) Rule: Right-Hand Thumb Rule.
37) Resistivity (ρ)
(a) R = ρ(l/A) → ρ = RA/l. SI Unit: Ωm.
(b) Alloys: Used in heating elements because they have high resistivity and don't oxidize at high temperatures.
38) Domestic Circuit Wiring
(a) Live: Red/Brown; Neutral: Black/Blue.
(b) I = P/V = 1000/220 = 4.54A. Use a 5A fuse.
(c) Earth Wire: Connects appliance body to earth; prevents shock during leakage.
OR: Precautions: 1. Proper earthing. 2. Using Fuse/MCB to prevent overloading.
39) Refraction and Lens/Mirror Formula
(a) Glass Slab: Parallel faces cause equal and opposite refraction. Normal incidence: ∠i = 0, ∠r = 0, no deviation.
(b) Concave Lens: u = -30, f = -20. 1/v = 1/f + 1/u = 1/-20 + 1/-30 = -5/60. v = -12 cm.
OR: (a) Snell's Law: sin i / sin r = constant (n).
(b) Convex Mirror: f = +3, u = -6. 1/v = 1/f - 1/u = 1/3 - (-1/6) = 3/6. v = +2.0 m.
